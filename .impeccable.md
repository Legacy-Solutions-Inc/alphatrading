## Design Context

### Users
Iloilo-based SME importers sourcing goods from China. They arrive skeptical, having seen cheap-looking logistics template sites and been burned or almost-burned by scammy freight forwarders. Primary context: evaluating on desktop or mobile while researching options before reaching out. They are business operators, not designers — they judge credibility through typography, photography, copy precision, and overall "does this look like a real company."

### Primary Job
**Vet legitimacy.** The #1 goal of every visitor is to decide whether Alpha Trade is a real, capable, trustworthy operator worth contacting. Conversion = a message sent or a quote requested, but the path to that action runs entirely through trust signals.

### Brand Personality
**Trustworthy + Modern.** Three words: *institutional, precise, contemporary.* Should feel like a freight forwarder with real infrastructure (think Flexport's restraint, not a localhost-tutorial template). Warm where it counts (human-scale copy, clear contact), rigorous where it matters (numbers, logos, specifics).

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Light mode default with confident navy anchoring. Avoid dark-mode-with-glowing-accents (reads as crypto/SaaS, not logistics).
- **Anti-references**: Bootstrap-era freight forwarder templates; clip-art cargo-ship illustrations; stock shipping icons; rainbow gradient heroes; "trust badges" that look fake.
- **Positive references**: Flexport, Maersk's marketing site, Freightos — institutional restraint with modern typography.
- **Color**: Navy primary (#0c3b6a) is set; use accent blue (#1a73e8) sparingly, reserve for CTAs and interactive elements only. Neutrals should carry most of the page.

### Design Principles
1. **Every pixel earns trust or it leaves.** Decorative flourishes that don't reinforce legitimacy actively hurt the page.
2. **Specifics beat superlatives.** "Weekly sailings Guangzhou→Iloilo, 14-day door-to-door" beats "Fast, reliable shipping."
3. **Photography over illustration.** Real containers, real warehouses, real people beat icon-and-heading cards.
4. **Typographic hierarchy does the heavy lifting.** This brand doesn't need gradients, glows, or glass — it needs precise type and confident spacing.
5. **Restraint signals competence.** An understated layout with dense, accurate information reads as a real company. A visually busy page reads as marketing-over-substance.
